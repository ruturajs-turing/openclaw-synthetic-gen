# September 1, 2024 — Nadia's Visit, Day Four

She left at noon and I've been sitting with the quiet of the apartment ever since. It's a different quiet than usual. My quiet is comfortable — deliberate, earned. This quiet has an edge to it.

Four days go fast when you're not wasting them. We did the Ferry Building on Saturday morning, elbowing through the farmers market crowd, which she loved and I tolerated because she loved it. She bought three jars of jam and a sourdough loaf the size of a small animal. I bought coffee and stood out of the way.

Napa on Sunday was the centerpiece. We mapped out three wineries — I'd done the research, she approved the itinerary — and spent the whole day moving between tasting rooms and the sunlit stretch of Highway 29. The first winery was fine, good Cab Franc, nothing revelatory. The second one was where it happened: a tiny family operation, maybe 800 cases total, and they were pouring a single-vineyard Pinot from a mountain site that tasted like something I'd been trying to describe for years. Nadia took one sip and said 'okay, that's it.' We bought six bottles between us and argued about who got the last two.

She got them. She was the guest.

Monday we argued. It was about something small — my habit of making decisions unilaterally without stating them as decisions. She said 'you just announce things.' I said I was being efficient. She said that's the same thing I always say. She's been saying this for fifteen years and she's probably right fifteen years running. We ate Thai food in silence and then talked for two hours after dinner about her daughters, her firm, a neighborhood in Johannesburg she wants to move to. The argument dissolved the way our arguments always do: into something more important.

Airport drop-off is a skill I have not developed. I said 'safe travels' and meant about forty other things. She hugged me for longer than usual and said 'come visit, Brandon. Actually come.' I said I would. I meant it this time, I think.

The apartment feels geometrically larger than it did four days ago. I'm going to go for a run.
