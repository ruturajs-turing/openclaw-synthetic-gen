# Reading Highlights — 2024

## Books

**"The Mountain" by Dougald MacDonald**
> *"Elevation doesn't give you answers. It gives you enough silence to stop avoiding the questions."*
Read this in March before the Tam hike. Underlined that line twice.

**"Why We Sleep" by Matthew Walker**
> *"The shorter your sleep, the shorter your life span."*
Started tracking sleep in November. This book is alarming and I can't stop recommending it to people who didn't ask.

**"The Timeless Way of Building" by Christopher Alexander**
> *"A building or a town will only be alive to the extent that it is governed by the timeless way."*
Engineering and philosophy collapsed into one book. Referenced it in a project presentation to mixed results.

## Articles

**"The Hidden Cost of Deferred Infrastructure Maintenance" — Engineering Today, April 2024**
> Key stat: *Deferring maintenance for one year on average costs 4–6x more to repair vs. address on schedule.* Sent this to my PM. Passive-aggressively.

**"Trail Running at 50+: What the Science Actually Says" — Outside Magazine**
> *"Cartilage adapts. The old advice to stop was wrong. Train smart, not less."*
Saved this to show my doctor.

**"Tasting Like a Winemaker" — Wine Spectator, August 2024**
> *"Forget the vocabulary. What does it remind you of? Start there."*
This is the permission I didn't know I was waiting for re: the pretentious notebook.

**"On Living Alone in Your 50s" — The Atlantic, October 2024**
> *"The solitude you once fled becomes the architecture of a life you didn't plan but quietly prefer."*
Read this three times. Said nothing about it to anyone.
